Overall

The analysis of 927 meals found mixed results. By and large, the companies do good job with overall calorie count: on average the meals have around 700 kcal which is just the recommendation for a single meal. Where the meals more often than not fail to meet the recommendations is the specific nutrients such as carbohydrates, protein and fat. All of the companies have much more protein and much less carbohydrates in their meals than recommended. The fat type considered to be unhealthy, saturated fat, varied a lot between the companies. “These meals seem to be following the hot nutritional trends that are going on now”, nutrition scientist Pamela Koch says. That is: lots of protein and less carbohydrates. Still, while most of the meals don’t fill all the recommendations for a healthy meal, compared to what Americans generally eat, these meals might be still a good or even better option. Most Americans don’t anyway eat in the way it is recommended by USDA.

Calories

Calorie-wise the companies did all very well. On average, one dinner had 690 kcal or 34 percent of the daily need. That’s just the amount the recommendations suggest for the average 2000 kcal daily diet. From a dinner, one should except to get 30-40 percent of the daily energy and other nutrients. Sun Basket had the least calories, 580, on average, and Marley Spoon the most, 770 calories. Of course, individual needs vary hugely. A sporty young man needs much more calories than an elderly, inactive lady. This analyzed is based on 2000 kcal diet, the same diet that is used in Nutrition Fact labels. It matters who you are cooking to. “I have two teenage boys and I can tell you, if I was serving these meals to them, the proper amount would be more to them a little bit less for me. They need lot more calories”, the nutrition scientist Pamela Koch says.

Protein

The meals contain much more protein than recommended. On average, meal kit customers get 74 percent of their daily need from one meal, which is about twice the protein that is recommended for one meal. Nine out of ten meals have more protein than recommended, and one out 15 meals are so rich in protein that they fulfill your whole daily need in just one meal.

Many health-conscious people are focusing on protein right now. According to Pamela Koch that is concerning. Too much protein accounts to extra stress for kidneys as the body has to metabolize it.

All the companies have exceeding amount of protein in their meal, the average raging from Sun Basket’s 70 percent to Blue Apron’s 79 percent.

Fat

On average, the meals had 42 percent of the maximum daily recommendation of fat. Sun Basket had the least fat (33 percent) and Marley Spoon the most (49 percent). However, to look only at the total fat doesn’t really tell you enough. Unsaturated fats are good for you, saturated not so. And saturated fat was what varied between companies.

Saturated Fat

There are huge differences in saturated fat level between the companies. That is, for those companies who disclose the information. Only Blue Apron, HelloFresh and Sun Basket reveal how much saturated fat is in their meals. Plated and Martha & Marley Spoon don’t.

Hello Fresh had the most saturated fat in their meal, 65 percent of the daily recommendation. That is almost twice as much as recommended for one meal. Four out of five of their meals had more than the recommend maximum of 6-8 grams of saturated fat per meal.

Sun Basket had the least amount of saturated fat, only 20 percent of the daily maximum per meal. About five out of six Sun Basket meals have good levels of saturated fat.

On average, Americans eat too much saturated fat. Two out of three Americans get too much saturated fat.

Fiber

TFiber, which you get mainly from whole wheat and vegetables, is good for example to the intestines.

All the companies have less fiber than recommended. On average the meals contained just 25 percent of the fiber needed daily. Sun Basket had the most fiber in their meal, 8 grams or 29 percent of the daily need. Blue Apron had the least, 6 grams or 21 percent. “Considering these are fresh foods I’d like the fiber content to be closer to 30-40. People might not be getting enough from their other meals during the day,” Pamela Koch says.

The recommendation for minimum amount of fiber you should get, lately rose couple of grams to 28 grams a day. On average, Americans eat only around 15 grams of fiber in a day . So even if the meal kits don’t fulfil the recommendations, compared to average American meals, meal kits don’t do any worse fiber wise.

Carbs

The meals are really skimp on carbohydrates. On averages, from one meal you get only 23 percent of the carbohydrates needed daily. Sun Basket has the least carbs, 17 percent, and Blue Apron the most, 25 percent.

Nowadays, many health-conscious people consider carbohydrates unnecessary. That’s not true, says Pamela Koch. Carbohydrates just have a bad reputation because of sugar.
“Complete carbohydrates and whole grain are actually really good way to get energy,” Koch says.

Salt

From the information the companies disclose it’s impossible to say how much salt customers get from their meals. Plated and Marley Spoon don’t tell their salt content at all. HelloFresh and SunBasket reveal only the amount of salt that comes with the ingredients, but they don’t take into account the salt customer adds as advised in the recipe.
“[..] it is difficult to quantify the amount our customers use,” Gloria Lam from Hello Fresh communications team said in an email in December.

Yet Blue Apron is able to calculate it. It is the only company that tells the total salt level of prepared food. And the figure is not flattering: from an average meal customer gets 55 percent of the daily recommendation. Four out of five of their meals have more salt per meal than recommended and one in five meals give you the double than recommended.
Studies show that Americans in general eat way too much salt. On average, women eat 2,980 mg of sodium and men eat 4,240 mg sodium in a day . Compared to that, Blue Apron meals, containing 1270 mg of sodium per meal on average, seem pretty typical.
